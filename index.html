MyTrailKG Mobile App

Udacity Nanodegree Project

The project I created in terms of Udacity Online Course. Students were suggested to choose any topic and create end-to-end design project usind different deliverables and design tools.

  • Role: Research, Wireframes, Prototyping, Testing, Sketching
  • Year: Spring, 2020
  • Tools: Figma, Miro, Zeplin

Overview and Problem Statement

'MyTrail' is a mobile application that is supposed to help people to choose destination of their journey and get prepared for it. The app should also serve as a great trip planner so the users could avoid the inconveniences and frustrations that might appear because of poorly- planned journey. In the initial stage of the project I created a scope where I defined subject (tourism), target audience (amateur hikers of any age) and purpose (hiking for leisure, not for professional purposes).
Challenge: Build a mobile application for amateur hikers who go hiking for leisure.

Research and Analysis

Within the whole design process, I was following the UCD (user-centered design) approach so my decisions were supported by evidences I gathered through deliverables and techniques I used in the project.
I started the project from the Research:
Method: Semi-structured interview
Participants: People who go hiking from time to time
Quant.: 4 people
The research is aimed at defining user’s behavior, needs and current pain points they have using similar products. The research findings helped me to determine what opportunities may exist for creating new digital product for planning hiking trips and how it is different from non-digital planning.
Some of the research findings:

Our new product could focus on including all the necessary data about the track, such as: maps, the weather, equipment, level of difficulty, distance, guides, photos, people’s reviews and tips and tricks.

Our product could focus on upgrading the map by adding “useful spots” to ease users’ navigation.

Synthesis

After the research was finished I started Synthesis process. I divided notes into chunks and then grouped them by themes. During this stage I used some techniques and frameworks for getting initial visual concepts, prioritizing features, such as Affinity mapping, Crazy8, Value vs Complexity Quadrat.
Also before starting ideate the features I reframed the insight statements (themes) into How Might We questions to turn those challenges into opportunities for design e.g. “How Might We provide more relevant information about the track and trip so hikers could more efficiently plan their journey”.

Hi-Fi and Lo-Fi Prototyping

The process of prototyping was divided into two parts: Low-Fidelity Prototyping and High-Fidelity Prototyping. I used Figma as a prototyping tool including its assets and plugins. Also the Atomic Design method helped me to put together the design system which I used for hi-fi prototyping. After the hi-fi prototype was created I prepared it for handoff by exporting Figma designs to Zeplin with the style guides and all the elements included.

Test: Validation, Usability, Feedback

Usability testing was one of the crucial parts of the design process. It was conducted remotely with the help of Usability guide. Five were involved in usability testing, they were supposed to follow the instructions and answer the questions. After competing usability test, some insights were discovered where the app is not entirely working well. I went through each session of Usability flow in Miro and mapped specific problems to areas where they were discovered. I noticed that users had problems with finding “map button” so I could draw my attention to where I should be addressing my design iteration.

Design: Iteration

As design is iterative process in the last step of the process it was important to review the project and look for issues and opportunities for improvement in accessibility and annotate the screens accordingly. This time I used Lookback platform for Usability Test. There were 10 participants who were asked to follow the provided instructions and give a feedback of their user flow. After the feedback was gathered some I made some amendments to make the design more usable.
